The Videogame Metamedium

Virtual worlds within videogames contain books, paintings, newspapers, radios and other media artifacts that take part in the gaming experience in different ways. These objects can be employed as just ornamental elements in the background but, in some cases, they function as fundamental pieces for the video game’s narrative and interactivity. In my master thesis, I tried to dissect this phenomenon both from a semiotics and game design point of view.

Written in 2019-2020.

The research was done on a dataset of videogames, both old and new, that contain media.

The games in the dataset were collected thanks to a group of video game enthusiasts who collaborated by pointing out and describing the cases of media in videogames they remembered.

From the final list, I selected the most interesting examples and I investigated them with various degrees of depth.

The essay also contains a general introduction to the concept of remediation, explained through examples coming from other media, like paintings.
